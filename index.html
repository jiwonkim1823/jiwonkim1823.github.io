Title of the research

Advised by Professor Yong Gyu Lee

abstract:

Activity Recognition from Acoustic Data with Apple Watch

Advised by Professor Karan Ahuja

This project utilizes the Apple FastVit model to recognize user activity on low-performance devices like Apple Watch. It aims to accurately determine user actions by processing audio data, setting the stage for the integration of IMU sensors to enhance activity detection precision. The final purpose is to achieve a multifaceted recognition system that interprets user activity with high accuracy while addressing the constraints of device performance and battery life.

Controlling Shareholders’ Tax Burden and Corporate Transparency

Research Proposal for the 'Studies in Tax Accounting' seminar, Seoul National University

This proposal, including hypotheses and basic research design, suggests that the tax burden on controlling shareholders of Korean chaebol firms (large conglomerates) can affect earnings management, the opacity of financial reporting, and the analyst information environment. It aims to use inheritance events in these firms to investigate whether personal tax burdens can obscure transparency.
