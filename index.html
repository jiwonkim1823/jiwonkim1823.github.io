Projects (research proposals from master’s course seminars)

Controlling Shareholders’ Tax Burden and Corporate Transparency

Research Proposal for the 'Studies in Tax Accounting' seminar, Seoul National University

This proposal, including hypotheses and basic research design, suggests that the tax burden on controlling shareholders of Korean chaebol firms (large conglomerates) can affect earnings management, the opacity of financial reporting, and the analyst information environment. It aims to use inheritance events in these firms to investigate whether personal tax burdens can obscure transparency.

Accounting Conservatism in R&D Investment

Research Proposal for the 'Accounting Modeling and Analysis' seminar, Seoul National University

This proposal, including hypotheses and basic research design, extends the Laux and Ray (2020) model to explore how managerial risk-taking incentives (vega) and accounting conservatism together influence R&D investment and innovation quality.
